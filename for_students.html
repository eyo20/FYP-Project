How PeerLearn Works for Students

1

Create Your Profile

Sign up and create your student profile. Add your contact number and change your name.

2

Search for Tutors

Browse through our database of qualified peer tutors. Filter by subject, expertise level, availability, and ratings to find your perfect match.

3

Submit a Tutoring Request

Send a request to your chosen tutor, specifying the course and topics you need help with. Include your preferred time slots to streamline scheduling.

4

Arrange and Attend the Session

Use PeerLearn’s messaging system to communicate with your tutor. Agree on session details (time, location, and topics), then meet for a personalized tutoring session tailored to yours IT coursework.

5

Settle Payment Offline

After the session, pay your tutor directly with offline payment as agreed upon.

6

Rate and Review

After your session, provide feedback and rate your tutor to help other students make informed decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose the right tutor?
You can browse tutor profiles, which include their academic background, subjects they teach, ratings from other students, and their teaching approach. Use our filters to narrow down tutors by subject and rating to find someone who matches your needs.
How much does tutoring cost?
Tutoring rates are set by individual tutors and vary based on subject complexity and tutor experience. Most peer tutors charge between RM 15-30 per hour, which is significantly more affordable than professional tutoring services.
Can I cancel a booked session?
Yes, you can cancel a session. Please note, however, that cancellations must be communicated to the tutor at least 24 hours before the scheduled time. This policy ensures that tutors have enough time to adjust their course schedules.
How do I communicate with my tutor?
you have access to our messaging system where you can communicate with your tutor, share materials, and discuss your learning needs before and after the session.
What if I'm not satisfied with my tutoring session?
Tutees can express dissatisfaction through ratings and official channels. Administrators mediate fairly to protect the rights and interests of both parties. Low-quality tutors are suspended to ensure service levels.
